London,It is difficult to speak adequately or justly of London.

John Stephens Clock Wed 19, 2021 5

Whether you're making London a destination city or using it as a launching pad to see the rest of Europe, this city will sate your appetite for adventures, sights, and good eats.

Regardless what part of town you spend the night there are things to do and see just outside your door. A must do for first timers is traveling on the Tube, accessible from all around the city. It's so much easier than trying to drive here, especially if you're used to driving on the right side of the road. This website is especially useful for finding routes: www.tfl.gov.uk Note that each route is color coded. If you are on a blue route according to your map, which can be gotten free inside the tube, then the handrails inside each "train" will be painted blue. This will help you confirm you've hopped on the right line. There are also graphics on each train that show you all the stops on that line. Zip around underground in comfort then pop up in the city's renown places, like Piccadilly Circus! Within walking distance from Piccadilly is the Soho district where you can find every imaginable international dish there is. Some of the best Indian food in the world is served here.

If you are a tight budget traveler, beware. One can find less expensive rooms than at the Ritz Carlton, but don't expect the great deals one finds in other parts of Europe. Pay less, get less is the bottom line. Planning ahead will better your chances of getting a good bargain.
